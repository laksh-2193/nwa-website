Why Our Work Matters

Bridging the Gap

Millions of children in India are silently struggling with learning differences. We are working to change that.

The Challenge

A Silent Crisis in India’s Mainstream Education System

India has over 350 million students in its education system. Of these, at least 35 million children[source] are likely to be silently struggling with learning challenges—not because they lack intellectual ability, but because they learn differently.

At the same time, classrooms are often under-resourced, with teachers supporting at least 50 students per class. Without awareness or training, many of these children are overlooked or misunderstood—often labelled as ‘slow’ or ‘careless.’

Over time, the gap widens. Children fall behind. Confidence erodes. Potential is lost.

Our Response

How We Are Making a Difference

Three interconnected ways we are bridging the gap.

One

Building Awareness

Helping schools, families, and communities understand that learning differences are real, common, and treatable—not a reflection of a child’s intelligence. Awareness is the first step in creating inclusive environments where every child can thrive.

Two

Equipping Educators

Training teachers in inclusive strategies so they can recognise and support neurodivergent learners within their everyday classroom practice. Practical, evidence-informed tools that work within real Indian classrooms.

Three

Supporting Children

Working directly with students through screening, assessment, and tailored interventions—ensuring every child gets the support they need to thrive, regardless of their learning profile.

Every child deserves to be understood. Learning differently is not a deficit—it is a difference that, when recognised and supported, can become a strength.
