Our Work

What We Do

Three interconnected areas that together create lasting change for neurodivergent learners across India.

Core Programme

Teacher Training

Our teacher training programme equips educators with the knowledge and practical strategies they need to identify and support neurodivergent learners in their classrooms.

We focus on dyslexia, dyscalculia, dyspraxia, ADHD and inclusive teaching strategies—giving teachers evidence-informed tools that they can apply immediately.

  • Understanding specific learning differences and their impact on learning
  • Practical classroom strategies for inclusive teaching
  • Recognising early signs of learning difficulties
  • Building Individual Education Plans (IEPs) for identified students
270+ Teachers trained across India
Early Identification

Screening Tools

Early identification is critical. The sooner a child’s learning difference is recognised, the sooner appropriate support can be put in place.

We use adapted screening and assessment tools to evaluate both attainment and cognition, identifying children who may be struggling with specific learning difficulties.

  • Screening across key year groups to catch learning differences early
  • Detailed assessments for students identified with specific difficulties
  • Tailored intervention programs for dyslexia, dyspraxia, dyscalculia, and ADHD
  • Tools adapted from UK-based practice for the Indian context
200+ Student assessments completed
Building Partnerships

Family Engagement

Supporting a neurodivergent child requires consistency between school and home. We work closely with parents and families to build understanding and collaboration.

Many families are encountering terms like dyslexia or ADHD for the first time. We provide guidance, reassurance, and practical strategies that parents can use at home to support their child’s learning journey.

  • Parent awareness sessions on learning differences
  • Guidance on supporting learning at home
  • Building collaboration between school and family
  • Reducing stigma and increasing understanding within communities
4,500+ Students supported
Our Approach

Programme of Support

A structured programme designed to work directly within schools across India, delivering measurable impact for students and teachers.

  • Screening across key year groups to identify learning differences early
  • Detailed assessments for students identified with specific learning difficulties
  • Tailored intervention programs for dyslexia, dyspraxia, dyscalculia, and ADHD
  • Teacher training workshops on inclusive classroom strategies
  • Family engagement sessions building home-school partnerships
