Why It Matters
Who We Are

India has over 350 million students in its education system. Of these, at least 35 million children[source] are likely to be silently struggling with learning challenges—not because they lack intellectual ability, but because they learn differently.

At the same time, classrooms are often under-resourced, with teachers supporting at least 50 students per class. Over time, the gap widens.

Our Goal

To empower teachers to support neurodivergent learners so every child can reach their potential.

Our Work

What We Do

Three interconnected areas that together create lasting change for neurodivergent learners.

Family Engagement

Support parents and build collaboration between home and school to ensure consistent, understanding environments for every child.

Teacher Training

Focus on dyslexia, dyscalculia, dyspraxia, ADHD and inclusive strategies—equipping educators with practical, evidence-informed tools.

Screening Tools

Assess attainment and cognition using adapted tools to identify learning differences early, when intervention is most effective.

Our Journey

A Journey of Purpose

Neurowise Academy Foundation was born from a personal journey—one that began in the United Kingdom and gradually found its purpose in India.

Founded by Amita Amlani, the organisation draws from her lived experience of supporting her two neurodivergent children. Navigating assessments, uncertainty, and advocacy led her to retrain as a Specialist Teacher and Assessor for Specific Learning Difficulties (SpLD).

After more than a decade working within the UK’s special educational needs system, her work is shaped by strong UK-based practice and international standards—adapted thoughtfully for India.

Today, the foundation works directly with students while equipping teachers, parents, and communities with tools to support neurodivergent learners.

Why Our Work Matters

Bridging the Gap

Across India, many children with learning differences are overlooked or misunderstood—often labelled as ‘slow’ or ‘careless.’

Most schools do not yet have access to early screening, trained specialists, or inclusive teaching practices, leaving students without the support they need to thrive.

Neurowise Academy Foundation works to bridge this gap by building awareness, equipping educators, and supporting children directly—creating pathways for every learner to be recognised, understood, and included.

Our Approach

Programme of Support

A structured programme designed to work directly within schools across India, delivering measurable impact for students and teachers.

  • Screening across key year groups to identify learning differences early
  • Detailed assessments for students identified with specific learning difficulties
  • Tailored intervention programs for dyslexia, dyspraxia, dyscalculia, and ADHD
  • Teacher training workshops on inclusive classroom strategies
  • Family engagement sessions building home-school partnerships
